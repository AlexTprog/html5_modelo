Jorge Basadre

The Historian of the Republic

Here is a timeline of Jorge Basadre's life:

  • 1903 - He was born in Tacna, Chile.
  • 1908 - He began his instruction at the Liceo Santa Rosa, a small, almost familiar school run by a Peruvian teacher and tolerated by the Chilean authorities, who had officially closed the Peruvian schools in 1900 for not adjusting them to the Chilean educational plans.
  • 1923 - He wrote with Pedro Zulen, the bibliographic bulletin of the San Marcos library.
  • 1927 - He remains imprisoned on the island of San Lorenzo on suspicion of conspiring against Leguía . Once free, he concludes his law studies and in the Faculty of Letters he supports his doctoral thesis "Contribution to the history of the social and political evolution of Peru during the Republic."
  • 1930 - Appointed as director of the library of Saint Mark.
  • 1935 - Conducts research on Indian law.
  • 1947 - Directs the reconstruction of the national library after a fire.
  • 1948 - He is appointed director of cultural affairs of the OAS.
  • 1958 - He is decorated by the Government of the German Federal Republic with the Order of Merit in the rank of First Class Grand Cross.
  • 1960 - Receives an OAS scholarship to finance his research in Washington. Post Materials for another abode.
  • 1968 - Post the 6th. augmented and corrected edition of History of the Republic of Peru.
  • 1979 - Receives the order of the Peruvian sun.
  • 1980 - He dies at 97, after being hospitalized for 4 days he dies on Sunday, June 29 at 7 in the morning. On the 30th, before heading to the El Ángel cemetery, his coffin was carried on his shoulders by the galleries of the old Hall of Degrees and the courtyard of the Faculty of Letters of the University of San Marcos.

"Jorge Basadre is considered by many to be the most important historian of the Republic, one of his most relevant books is The Promise of Peruvian Life, the first edition of which dates back three quarters of a century."

-- Peruvian Sociologist Roberto Rodríguez Rabanal
